History of the NHL

The Founding (1917)

The National Hockey League was founded on November 26, 1917, in Montreal, Quebec, after the suspension of its predecessor, the National Hockey Association (NHA). The original teams were the Montreal Canadiens, Montreal Wanderers, Ottawa Senators, and the Toronto Arenas (who would later become the Maple Leafs).

The "Original Six" Era (1942–1967)

From 1942 to 1967, the NHL consisted of only six teams:

  • Boston Bruins
  • Chicago Black Hawks
  • Detroit Red Wings
  • Montreal Canadiens
  • New York Rangers
  • Toronto Maple Leafs

This period, known as the "Original Six" era, was a time of stability and intense, historic rivalries, particularly between teams like the Canadiens and the Maple Leafs.

The Expansion Era (1967–Present)

The NHL underwent its largest expansion in 1967, doubling its size from six to twelve teams. This marked the beginning of a new era of growth and change.

Expansion Rounds

  1. The "Great Expansion" of 1967 added six new teams:
    • Los Angeles Kings
    • Minnesota North Stars
    • Oakland Seals
    • Philadelphia Flyers
    • Pittsburgh Penguins
    • St. Louis Blues
  2. The 1970 expansion added two teams:
    • Buffalo Sabres
    • Vancouver Canucks
  3. The 1972 expansion added two teams:
    • New York Islanders
    • Atlanta Flames
  4. The 1974 expansion added two teams:
    • Washington Capitals
    • Kansas City Scouts
  5. The 1979 expansion added four teams from the WHA:
    • Edmonton Oilers
    • Hartford Whalers
    • Quebec Nordiques
    • Winnipeg Jets
  6. The 1990s and 2000s saw further expansion:
    • San Jose Sharks (1991)
    • Tampa Bay Lightning (1992)
    • Ottawa Senators (1992)
    • Florida Panthers (1993)
    • Nashville Predators (1998)
    • Atlanta Thrashers (1999)
    • Columbus Blue Jackets (2000)
    • Minnesota Wild (2000)
  7. Recent expansions to balance the West:
    • Vegas Golden Knights (2017)
    • Seattle Kraken (2021)
    • Utah Mammoth (2024)
